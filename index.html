air force 1

Nike Air Force 1 is a popular sneaker model from Nike. It was first released in 1982 and designed by Bruce Kilgore. The shoe was named after the Air Force One aircraft that carries the President of the United States. The Air Force 1 was the first basketball shoe to feature Nike Air technology. The sneaker has been released in many different colorways and collaborations with other brands and artists. It is available in low to high-top versions.

Style

low

The Nike Air Force 1 Low "Legend Blue" is a stylish shoe with a Black-based upper with Blue Legend accents, a translucent icy Blue midsole, and a solid Black outsole. Available for $70 USD on Nike.com, it features a Black-based upper with Blue Legend accents.
The Nike Air Force 1 is available in a Black, Smoke Grey, and Pure Platinum color scheme, featuring a Platinum upper, Smoke Grey sides, Black on Swooshes and rear, Black on tongues and laces, and Red tongue branding and heel tabs. Retailed at $110 USD.
Bruce Kilgore's iconic Air Force 1 silhouette has been popular since 1982, featuring numerous colorways and collaborations with Virgil Abloh and Travis Scott. The Nike Air Force 1 Green Gold Swoosh, the latest rendition of the model, is gaining attention among avid shoe collectors due to its popularity and collaborations with other brands.

mid

The Nike Air Force 1 Mid is set to launch in 'Midnight Navy' for Fall. The mid-top shoe features Navy leather upper, perforations on the toe, white leather Swoosh logos on panels, mini Swoosh and tongue label, and white midsole and rubber outsole. The color scheme is complemented by perforations on the toe.
Nike Sportswear has released a new Air Force 1 Mid in 'Bronze Eclipse' for Fall, available in Gum Dark Brown, Bronze Eclipse, Light British Tan, and Noble Red colors. The shoes feature winter-ready materials like thick suede and coated synthetics, and a Gum rubber outsole. The shoes are available at select retailers, including Nike.com, with a retail price of $115.
Nike Sportswear's "Color of the Month" Pack continues with classic Air Force 1 mid colorways, including the Baby Blue edition. The shoe features a full Baby Blue nubuck upper with Summit White detailing, tongue, insole, back heel, midsole, and rubber Baby Blue outsole. Retail price is $140 USD.

HIGH

Nike Sportswear's "Color of the Month" Pack continues with classic Air Force 1 High colorways, including the Baby Blue edition. The shoe features a full Baby Blue nubuck upper with Summit White detailing, tongue, insole, back heel, midsole, and rubber Baby Blue outsole. Retail price is $140 USD.
The Nike Air Force 1 High has introduced an "over-branded" look, borrowing from the Air Force 1 Low. The High features a tonal leather upper with an "Olive Aura" arrangement on the adjustable ankle strap. The color scheme is reminiscent of military garb, with "Total Orange" paired with a drab green hue. The orange shade, also seen on the Air Max 90, further reinforces the over-branding on the laces and lateral heel. White midsoles and tread nod to the Air Force 1's heritage.
Nike has unveiled a two-toned version of its Air Force 1 High, featuring signature Swoosh colors of "Grey Fog" and "Neptune Green". The two-toned design is paired with tumbled leather constructions and lighter pigments of grey and white, allowing the planet-inspired tone to take over areas like the Swooshes, dubraes, tongues, and heels. This collaboration with Don C and Matthew Williams' 1017 ALYX 9SM has garnered significant attention. The clean two-toned execution is expected to maintain the pair's popularity.
